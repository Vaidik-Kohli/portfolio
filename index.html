About

I started with Python because I wanted to make something that moved on screen, not just print lines in a console. That led me into Pygame, then into web work when I wanted a place to document what I was building.

Most of what I know comes from trial and error. I read docs, test ideas, and keep notes when a bad decision teaches me something useful. That habit helped me move faster than I expected.

Outside code, I spend time on cricket, photography, and games. Those three shape how I think about timing, composition, and feedback loops in projects.

  • Primary stack: Python, Pygame, HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Tools: Git, Figma, Canva, Photoshop
  • Focus: projects that feel complete, not half demos
  • Location: Amritsar, Punjab, India

Projects

Two builds that took real iteration, not one-night prototypes.

Slot Machine Game

Python • Pygame

I made this to learn game architecture in a way tutorials never forced me to. The game has four risk levels, a jackpot system, 12 achievements, an aura mechanic, particle bursts, and sound cues that match the pace of each spin.

The hard part was balancing reward and frustration so it felt fair. I rewrote parts of the random logic and state handling a few times before it stopped feeling noisy and started feeling intentional.
